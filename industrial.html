THE PROBLEM

The owner's engineer builds the EPC shortlist during pre-FEED. You're not invited to that meeting.

Industrial megaproject procurement — semiconductor fabs, LNG export terminals, EV gigafactories — begins with FEED (Front-End Engineering Design), and before that, with pre-FEED. During pre-FEED, the owner's engineering firm establishes project parameters, technical requirements, and — critically — begins evaluating which EPCs have the capability and capacity to execute.

By the time the owner awards the FEED contract and the GC begins issuing subcontractor bid invites, the preferred EPC shortlist has been assembled through 12–18 months of informal technical evaluation and relationship building. The contractors on that list got there by being in front of the owner's engineer during pre-FEED — not by responding to a formal solicitation.

The public signals that predict this window are specific and early. CHIPS Act grant announcements from NIST and DOE precede ground-breaking by 18–24 months. EPA Title V air permit applications are filed 12–18 months before construction. FERC gas pipeline certificate applications signal LNG terminal construction timelines 18–30 months out. Each of these signals predicts a multi-hundred-million-dollar EPC procurement event with high confidence.

Most mid-tier industrial EPCs don't read federal grant announcements. They don't monitor EPA permit databases. They attend industry conferences and maintain relationships with GCs they've worked with before. That works when the GC calls them. It doesn't work when they need to be on the owner's engineer's pre-FEED shortlist 18 months before the GC is selected.

METHODOLOGY

The Signal Engine. Three steps.

01

Signal Monitoring

ContractMotion monitors CHIPS Act grant announcements from NIST and DOE, EPA Title V air permit applications, FERC gas pipeline certificate filings, MARAD LNG export approvals, DOE conditional loan commitments, and county industrial site permit applications. Every signal is indexed by facility type, location, developer, and estimated construction timeline.

When a CHIPS Act grant fires for a specific fab location — and EPA files a Title V application at the same site — and a utility interconnection request appears for 300+ MW at the adjacent substation — that is a confirmed pre-FEED signal. Owner's engineer conversations are happening now.

02

Contact Mapping

Once a signal cluster identifies a qualifying project, ContractMotion maps the procurement chain. The owner's engineering firm retained for pre-FEED. The specific project director and discipline leads. The owner's construction program manager. We identify who is evaluating EPCs during pre-FEED and when that window closes.

03

Pre-FEED Positioning

ContractMotion positions your firm into the owner's engineer's pre-FEED evaluation. Outreach references the specific project's parameters — facility type, power load, state, and developer — and your firm's comparable completed work at the relevant scale and discipline.

Getting onto the owner's engineer's pre-FEED EPC shortlist is a technical qualification process that happens 12–18 months before any public solicitation. We initiate it before the evaluation closes.
