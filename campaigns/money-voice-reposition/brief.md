# ContractMotion — Money-Voice Repositioning Brief

**Decision (2026-05-26):** tiered single brand · hybrid vertical architecture · permit engine folds in.
**The shift:** current copy sells the *mechanism* (signal intel, pre-RFP) to a rational procurement buyer. We re-layer it to sell the *money outcome + status* to an ambitious, already-winning owner — Jeremy Haynes / Megladon register. Same product. Different emotional layer.

> We are NOT helping poor people or tiny businesses. We speak to the owner doing real numbers who wants to dominate. The copy must repel the one-truck shop and flatter the market leader.

### Headlines (pick the tier-leader; mechanism becomes the subhead)
1. ★ **"You're already the name in your market. Your pipeline doesn't act like it."** — status/identity. Flatters the winner, implies a gap only they can close.
2. "You didn't build a $7M company to fight 8 bidders for table scraps." — contrarian/enemy. Greed + identity jolt.

## The money-voice rubric (so it stays on-brand)
1. **Lead with money + status, not mechanism.** Revenue numbers ($, not "more leads").
2. **Speak to the already-winning owner.** "You're winning — here's how you dominate." Never "let us help you grow."
3. **Disqualify down-market explicitly.** One line that repels the one-truck shop and flatters the leader.
4. **Identity/ambition hooks.** "You didn't build $X to settle for $Y."
5. **Bold, money-framed guarantee** (results/pay-on-performance) — but at the close, never the hero.
6. **Operator peer voice.** Contractions, punch-then-breathe rhythm. No corporate, no AI tells.
7. **Avoid the saturated claims** (per positioning.md): "more/better leads," "AI-powered outbound," "we book meetings," "win more bids," "find your next project."
