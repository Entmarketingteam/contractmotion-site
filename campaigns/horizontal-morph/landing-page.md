### PROBLEM

You see the RFP. You bid it. You lose.

Not because your pricing was off. Because three names were already on it before you saw the page. Someone else's team knew the contract was forming six months ago. They were already in the building. They were the buyer's first call.

The RFP wasn't a competition. It was paperwork for a decision already made.

Here's the math nobody talks about: when you're the 4th bidder introduced to a commercial decision-maker, your close rate is 11%. When you're the 1st name surfaced pre-procurement, it's 53%. Same crew. Same pricing. Five times the close rate, just by being earlier.

The contracts you're losing aren't lost on price. They're lost on timing.

---

### SOLUTION

Two things have to live in the same place for this to work.

**Signal.** Knowing which contracts are forming, in which counties, for which buyers, before they hit any bid board. Permits get filed. Liens get recorded. Lots get purchased. Capex gets announced. Agencies budget. Set-asides get reserved. Every contract leaves a paper trail 30 to 180 days before procurement opens. You just have to know which trail to read.

**Motion.** Sequences that introduce you at the exact moment the project leaves "considering" and enters "shortlisting." Not generic cold email. Outbound that references the permit you just saw filed, the expansion you just saw announced, the incumbent contract you just saw expire.

Most tools do one or the other. Database vendors hand you signal and disappear. Lead-gen agencies execute outbound but don't know your vertical's plumbing. ContractMotion is the seam between them — signal stack tuned per vertical, outbound calibrated to each signal type.

### OBJECTIONS

**"We already use [ConstructConnect / BidPrime / GovWin / Apollo]."**
Those tools give you data. We hand you data + the outbound that introduces you to the buyer attached to it. The data is the raw material. We're the finished product.

**"Why not just hire a generic lead-gen agency?"**
Because cold email about "your construction business" gets ignored. Cold email referencing the permit you filed last Tuesday gets replies. Generic agencies don't know your verticals' signals. We do.

**"My salespeople already do this manually."**
Then ask them how much of their week is spent finding the signals vs. acting on them. We turn 30 hours of research into 30 minutes of decision-making.

**"How is this different from buying a list?"**
A list tells you who exists. Signal tells you who's about to move. Motion turns "about to move" into "about to take your call."
