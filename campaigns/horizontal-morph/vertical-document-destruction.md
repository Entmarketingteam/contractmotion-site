## PROBLEM

One 50-site healthcare contract adds $400K–$800K in ARR to a regional shredding operator. Most operators never see the contract until RFP day. By then, the incumbent has been quietly renewed by the procurement officer, or the new vendor was selected three months ago through a relationship the operator wasn't part of.

You're a quote in a folder. You're not in the conversation.

The shredding industry runs on multi-year contracts that renew on predictable cycles. The operators winning the big accounts know the cycle. They time their outbound to land in the renewal window — 90 to 180 days before contract expiration, when the buyer is reviewing options but hasn't committed yet.

## THE SIGNAL STACK

What we monitor for document destruction operators:

- **Healthcare facility expansion** — CMS Certificate of Need filings, hospital system M&A activity, new facility permits (new facility = new shredding RFP)
- **Hospital system mergers** — vendor consolidation events trigger contract renegotiation
- **HHS/HIPAA audit activity** — facilities under audit scrutiny tighten compliance and may revisit vendor contracts
- **State procurement records** — public contract expiration tracking for government accounts
- **Financial institution branch activity** — branch closures, mergers, FDIC enforcement actions
- **Federal contract expiration** — SAM.gov tracking for federal shredding contracts (NAICS 561990)

## OBJECTIONS

**"We have a sales team that handles this."**
Your sales team is great at closing the meetings they get into. We're upstream — making sure they get into the right meetings 60–120 days earlier than they otherwise would.

**"Healthcare procurement is too relationship-driven."**
Exactly. The signal isn't a substitute for the relationship — it's the reason for the first conversation. "Saw your new facility permit" gets a meeting. "Want to talk shredding?" doesn't.

**"We're a smaller regional operator."**
Smaller operators win this play more, not less. National vendors (Iron Mountain, Shred-it) bid on volume and inertia. Regional operators win on timing and white-glove. Signal gives you the timing.
