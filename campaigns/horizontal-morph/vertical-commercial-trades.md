## PROBLEM

73% of commercial trade contracts go to the 1st or 2nd bidder introduced to the decision-maker. By the time the RFP hits a public bid board, you're #4 — bidding for the slot that exists because procurement requires three quotes, not because your firm is being seriously considered.

You can be the cheapest crew with the best safety record and you still lose. The decision was made before procurement opened.

The contractors winning consistently aren't winning on bid. They're winning on timing.

---

## THE SIGNAL STACK

What we monitor for commercial trades:

- **Commercial building permits** — OpenGov county feeds, large-square-footage filings, MEP-tagged permits
- **Bid boards (cross-referenced)** — BidPrime, ConstructConnect, Procore Network, government procurement portals
- **Facility expansion filings** — REIT 10-Qs (Prologis, Public Storage, hospital systems), retail capex announcements
- **Lien activity** — CourtListener, county UCC filings (signals contractor distress + replacement opportunity)
- **Healthcare construction** — CMS Certificate of Need filings (state-level), hospital expansion permits
- **Retail rollouts** — Brand site selection announcements, ground-lease filings

## THE MOTION

3-touch sequence per signal:

- **Day 0** — Permit-trigger: "Saw the permit pulled at [facility]. Who's your MEP on it?"
- **Day 5** — Case study: similar facility type, what we did for [comparable client], specific outcomes
- **Day 10** — Decision-maker intro: facility-manager or owner-rep coffee offer

For multi-site signals (REIT capex, retail rollout): different cadence — leads with "we saw [Brand] is planning [N] new locations; here's the pattern for getting on the GC's preferred-sub list."

## OBJECTIONS

Existing GC pipeline + signal-driven outbound to facility owners = two top-of-funnel sources. Doesn't replace the GC channel.

**"How is this different from ConstructConnect?"**
ConstructConnect tells you a project exists. We tell you the project is coming, who the decision-maker is, and run the outbound to introduce you.
