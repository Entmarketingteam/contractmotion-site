## PROBLEM

The typical Texas custom builder closes 8–14 homes a year at $4–8M each. Lose 3 of those to a competitor who got there first and you've left $20M+ on the table. Not because your work was inferior. Because someone else's name came up first when the homeowner asked their HOA, their architect, or their realtor.

The homeowner's decision happens before the permits are pulled. By the time the project hits "active permit," it's already in someone's CRM.

Unless it's in yours first.

---

## THE SIGNAL STACK (vertical-specific)

What we monitor for custom-home builders:

- **TDLR registrations** (TX) — new builder licenses + project registrations
- **County building permits** — OpenGov, Travis, Williamson, Harris, Collin, Denton, Tarrant
- **Lot purchase filings** — deed records, especially residential lots in builder-preferred neighborhoods
- **HOA-approved builder lists** — track which HOAs add/drop names
- **CourtListener liens** — flags builders in distress (your opportunity to recover those clients)
- **Architectural firm activity** — public project filings, architect-builder pairings
