## PROBLEM

The average federal services RFP has 8.2 bidders. By the time a solicitation hits SAM.gov, the contracting officer has already had relationship calls with 2–3 firms who knew the requirement was coming. Those firms shaped the RFP language. They're the ones who win.

If your capture work starts when the RFP posts, you're not capturing — you're proposal-writing for a contract someone else has already captured. That's how mid-tier govcon firms grind down win rates from 40% to 8% over a few years. Not because they got worse. Because they're showing up later in the cycle.

## THE SIGNAL STACK

What we monitor for govcon services contractors:

- **SAM.gov + FedConnect** — solicitation pre-notices, sources sought, RFI activity (the 6-month lookahead)
- **USAspending.gov** — agency budget actuals vs. plan (overspend = supplemental coming; underspend = end-of-FY scramble)
- **Agency budget filings** — congressional budget justifications, OMB passbacks, agency strategic plans
- **Set-aside designations** — 8(a), WOSB, SDVOSB, HUBZone reservations on upcoming solicitations
- **Incumbent contract expirations** — FPDS.gov data, option period tracking, recompete windows
- **Program office staffing** — LinkedIn signal on contracting officer + program manager changes (reorg = recompete)
- **GAO + IG reports** — flag agencies under pressure to recompete or restructure contracts

## THE MOTION

Capture-cycle sequences by signal type:

- **Sources sought / RFI** — "Saw [agency] posted sources sought for [program]. Are you submitting a response, or is this on your radar but not yet on the calendar?"
- **Incumbent expiration** — "[Incumbent]'s contract on [program] expires in 11 months. The capture window opens now. Want our brief on what's changed in agency priorities since last award?"
- **Budget signal** — "[Agency] is overspending FY[X] by [Y]%. Supplemental contracts on [function] are coming. Worth talking?"
- **Set-aside match** — "Saw [agency] reserved [program] for [8(a) / SDVOSB / etc.]. Your NAICS matches. We have agency-PM context on this one."

## OBJECTIONS

**"We already use GovWin / GovTribe / Bloomberg Government."**
Those tools surface the data. We pair the data with the capture outbound — meaning we don't just tell you a contract is coming, we run the program-office and incumbent-displacement outreach for you. Different layer.

**"Our BD team does this manually."**
Then ask them how many sources-sought responses they file per quarter vs. how many they should. Manual govcon BD scales to about 12 active captures per FTE. We make 40+ active captures tractable.

**"We don't want spammy outreach to contracting officers."**
Neither do we. Govcon outbound is referential — "saw [specific filing]" or "noticed [specific signal]" — not generic. Done wrong it kills your past performance. Done right it's just informed BD.

**"We're a small business."**
Small business is the leverage point. Set-aside reservations are public. Matching your NAICS + size to upcoming set-asides is exactly the signal play.
